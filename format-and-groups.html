World Cup 2026 Format Explained

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the first with 48 teams — a major change from the 32-team format used since 1998. Here is how it all works, from the group stage to the final.

The new structure

  • 48 teams split into 12 groups of four (Groups A–L).
  • Each team plays 3 group games.
  • The top two from every group, plus the 8 best third-placed teams, advance — that's 32 teams into the knockouts.
  • A new Round of 32 begins the knockout phase, followed by the Round of 16, quarter-finals, semi-finals and the final.
  • 104 matches in total (up from 64).

Host cities

16 cities across three nations stage matches:

  • USA (11): Atlanta, Boston, Dallas, Houston, Kansas City, Los Angeles, Miami, New York/New Jersey, Philadelphia, San Francisco Bay Area, Seattle
  • Mexico (3): Mexico City, Guadalajara, Monterrey
  • Canada (2): Toronto, Vancouver

The opening match is at Estadio Azteca in Mexico City, and the final at MetLife Stadium near New York on 19 July 2026.
