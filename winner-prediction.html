Who Will Win the World Cup 2026?

Updated 2 June 2026 • 12 min read • Outright prediction

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is the most open in memory. With 48 teams and a brand-new format, more nations than ever have a realistic route to the latter stages. Here is our full winner prediction — the favourites, the value picks, the dark horses, and the team our model rates highest to lift the trophy on 19 July.

The favourites

France head our list. A ruthless blend of pace, depth and big-tournament experience makes them the team to beat, and their squad depth is uniquely suited to a long 104-match tournament where rotation matters. They reached the final in 2022 and the core is still in its prime.

Argentina, the defending champions, remain a serious threat. Tournament football rewards a settled, confident group, and few sides carry more belief. The question is whether an ageing spine can sustain its level across a longer competition in North American heat.

Brazil arrive with arguably the deepest attacking talent pool on the planet. If their defensive structure holds, they have the individual quality to win any single knockout tie.

Prices move fast at the top of the market — check the current outright odds for the favourites before backing any team.

Our model's one-line verdict: France are the most complete side, but the gap to Argentina, Brazil, England and Spain is the smallest it has been in years.

The European chasing pack

England finally look like a tournament team rather than a collection of names — a generation of elite attackers now backed by genuine midfield control. Spain's possession game travels well and their young core has only improved since their recent successes. Portugal and Germany both have the talent to win it on their day but bigger question marks over consistency.

Dark horses to watch

The expanded format opens the door for surprises. The nations we think offer the most value relative to their price:

  • Netherlands — a balanced side that always seems to find a deep run when underrated.
  • Croatia / Morocco — recent tournaments proved both can outperform their odds with elite organisation.
  • USA (host) — home advantage, familiar conditions and an improving young squad make a run to the latter stages plausible.
  • Uruguay — a tactically mature group capable of frustrating any favourite.

Golden Boot contenders

The longer tournament means more games and more chances for a prolific striker to run up a tally. The forwards we rate most likely to finish as top scorer are the spearheads of the highest-scoring sides — typically the main strikers of France, Argentina, Brazil and England, who combine penalty duties with the most goalscoring opportunities. Value often lies with a forward from a team expected to reach the semi-finals rather than the outright favourite.

Can a host nation win it?

Home advantage is real: familiar stadiums, no long-haul travel and partisan crowds. The USA is the most likely of the three hosts to make a deep run given its squad depth, while Mexico traditionally thrives at home and Canada arrives with its strongest-ever generation. Winning the whole thing would be a major upset — but reaching the quarter-finals is a credible target for the USA.

Our pick to win the World Cup 2026

Weighing squad depth, form, route and the demands of a 48-team tournament, our model's single most likely champion is France — narrowly ahead of Argentina and Brazil. But "most likely" still means roughly a one-in-six chance: in a field this deep, the smart approach is to respect the value in the chasing pack rather than assume the favourite cruises through.
