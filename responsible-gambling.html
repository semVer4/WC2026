Responsible Gambling

Betting should be fun, never a way to make money or solve financial problems. This page explains how to stay in control and where to get help.

18+
Adults only. Gambling is restricted to those of legal age (18+ in most countries, 21+ in some regions) and may be illegal where you live. Always check your local laws.

Stay in control

  • Set a budget you can comfortably afford to lose — and stick to it.
  • Set time and deposit limits with your operator.
  • Never chase losses or bet to win back money.
  • Don't bet when upset, stressed or under the influence.
  • Keep betting a small part of your entertainment — not a priority.

Warning signs

Consider taking a break if you are spending more than you planned, hiding your betting from others, borrowing money to bet, or feeling anxious about it. These are signs it may be becoming a problem.

Where to get help

  • GamCare (UK) — gamcare.org.uk
  • BeGambleAware — begambleaware.org
  • National Council on Problem Gambling (US) — ncpgambling.org, call/text 1-800-GAMBLER
  • Gambling Therapy (worldwide) — gamblingtherapy.org

Most licensed operators also offer self-exclusion and cooling-off tools — use them.

If gambling stops being fun, stop. Help is available, and reaching out is a sign of strength.
